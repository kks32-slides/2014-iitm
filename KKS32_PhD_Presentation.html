Lattice Boltzmann - Multiple Relaxation Time

  • The mesoscopic variables can be obtained as:

    • $\mbox{Density, }\rho=\sum\limits_{i}f_{\textit{i}} \mbox {; Velocity, } u=\frac{1}{\rho}\sum\limits_{i}\textit{e}_{\textit{i}}f_{\textit{i}}\\ \mbox{Pressure, }\mathbf{p}=C_s^2\rho$

  • $C_s$ is the lattice speed: $\Delta x / \Delta t$, where $\Delta x$ is the lattice size

Summary

  • MPM estimates longer run-out distances, where collisional regime predominates
  • MPM and DEM methods estimate similar run-out distances for large deformation problems
  • Change in reduction in radii from 0.7R to 0.95R reduces the permeability by a factor of 10
  • Hydrodynamic forces results in smaller run-out distances for collapse in fluid in comparison with dry collapse
  • The runout distance increases with decrease in permeability
  • Critical time for a collapse in fluid increases with decrease in permeability.
  • Low density sediments with lower permeability tends to flow longer due to hydroplaning and lubrication effects.
